Adhesion

\begin{align*} W_{12} = \gamma_1 + \gamma_2 - \gamma_{12} \end{align*}

The work of adhesion is the free energy required to sever the interface between two materials.

For the case of an interface between water and n-octane, the work of adhesion is 42.78 mJ\,m-2. Interestingly, this is roughly the same as the cohesive free energy of n-octane.

\begin{align*} \gamma_{GS} &= \gamma_{LS} + \gamma_{GL}\cos\theta_c \end{align*}

This is known as the Young-Dupre equation. Note that this expression puts limits on the possible relative values that the interfacial tensions can have, due to the fact that they must all be positive and that the value of the cosine can only vary between \(-1\) and \(1\).
