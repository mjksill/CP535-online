Quantum mechanics

  • The state of a system is characterized by a function (abstract vector): the wave function. For a system of \(N\) particles: \(\psi({\bf r}_1, {\bf r}_2,\dots,{\bf r}_N)\)
  • The wave function is a continuous and smooth function of the particle positions.
  • Joint probability distribution that a particle is at position \({\bf r}_1\), \({\bf r}_2\), …, \({\bf r}_N\) is
\begin{align*} P({\bf r}_1, {\bf r}_2,\dots,{\bf r}_N) &\propto |\psi({\bf r}_1, {\bf r}_2,\dots,{\bf r}_N)|^2 \\ &= \psi^*({\bf r}_1, {\bf r}_2,\dots,{\bf r}_N) \psi({\bf r}_1, {\bf r}_2,\dots,{\bf r}_N) \end{align*}
  • The properties of the system are determined by operators on the wave function.

Observables and operators

Operators map a function (state) to another function (state)

\begin{equation*} \hat{A} \psi({\bf r}) = \phi({\bf r}) \end{equation*}

Average value:

\begin{equation*} \langle A\rangle = \int d{\bf r} \psi^*({\bf r}) \hat{A} \psi({\bf r}) %= \frac{\langle\psi|\hat{A}|\psi\rangle}{\langle\psi|\psi\rangle} \end{equation*}

Eigenstates of \(\hat{A}\):

\begin{equation*} \hat{A}\psi_\alpha({\bf r}) = A_\alpha \psi_\alpha({\bf r}) \end{equation*}

Momentum operator

\begin{align*} \hat{p}_z \psi(z) = -i\hbar \frac{\partial}{\partial z} \psi(z) \qquad\qquad \hat{\bf p} \psi({\bf r}) = -i\hbar \nabla \psi({\bf r}) \end{align*}

Schrodinger equation

Hamiltonian:

\begin{equation*} \hat{H} = \left[- \frac{\hbar^2}{2m} \nabla^2 + V({\bf r}) \right] \end{equation*}

Time independent Schr\"odinger equation:

\begin{equation*} \left[- \frac{\hbar^2}{2m} \nabla^2 + V({\bf r}) \right]\psi({\bf r}) = E \psi({\bf r}) \end{equation*}

where \(V({\bf r})\) is the potential energy.

Time dependent Schr\"odinger equation:

\begin{equation*} \frac{\partial}{\partial t} \psi({\bf r},t) = - \frac{i}{\hbar}\hat{H}\psi({\bf r},t) \end{equation*}

Mixed states

  • Eigenfunctions and eigenvalues of \(\hat{A}\):

\(\psi_\alpha({\bf r})\) and \(A_\alpha\)

  • The wave function can be expanded in terms of eigenfunctions
\begin{align*} \psi({\bf r}) = \sum_\alpha c_\alpha \psi_\alpha({\bf r}) \end{align*}
  • Probability of being in state \(\alpha\): \(|c_\alpha|^2\)

Free particle

  • Schr\"odinger equation
\begin{equation*} -\frac{\hbar^2}{2m} \frac{\partial^2 \psi(z)}{\partial z^2} = E \psi(z) \end{equation*}
  • Wave function
\begin{equation*} \psi_k(z) = (2\pi)^{-1/2} e^{ikz} \end{equation*}
  • Momentum: \(p_z = \hbar k\)
  • Energy: \(\displaystyle E_k = \frac{\hbar^2 k^2}{2m} = \frac{p_z^2}{2m}\)

Particle in a box

  • Schr\"odinger equation
\begin{equation*} -\frac{\hbar^2}{2m} \frac{\partial^2 \psi(z)}{\partial z^2} = E \psi(z) \end{equation*}

with boundary conditions

\begin{equation*} \psi(0) = 0 \qquad\mbox{and}\qquad \psi(L)=0 \end{equation*}
  • Wave function
\begin{equation*} \psi_k(z) = \left(\frac{2}{L}\right)^{1/2} \sin\left(\frac{\pi n z}{L}\right) = \left(\frac{2}{L}\right)^{1/2} \sin kz \end{equation*}

where \(n=0\), \(1\), \(2\),…

  • Energy
\begin{equation*} E_k = \frac{\hbar^2}{2m} \left(\frac{\pi n}{L}\right)^2 = \frac{\hbar^2 k^2}{2m} \end{equation*}

where \(k^2=k_x^2+k_y^2+k_z^2\).

Restrictions on the wave function

The wave function must be smooth:

\begin{align*} \psi(0^-) &= \psi(0^+) \qquad \psi'(0^-) = \psi'(0^+) \\ \psi(d^-) &= \psi(d^+) \qquad \psi'(d^-) = \psi'(d^+) . \end{align*}

Solution:

\begin{align*} A &= \frac{2k_0(k+k_0) e^{-ikd}}{4kk_0\cos kd- 2i(k^2+k_0^2)\sin kd} \\ B &= \frac{2k_0(k-k_0) e^{+ikd}}{4kk_0\cos kd - 2i(k^2+k_0^2)\sin kd} \\ r &= \frac{(k_0^2-k^2)\sin kd}{(k^2+k_0^2)\sin kd + 2ikk_0\cos kd} \\ t &= \frac{2i kk_0e^{-ik_0d}}{(k^2+k_0^2)\sin kd + 2ikk_0\cos kd} \end{align*}
