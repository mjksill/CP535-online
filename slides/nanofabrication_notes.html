Scanning tunneling microscopy (STM)

  • Tunneling current between sample and tip changes exponentially (pA-nA) with the tip-sample distance
  • Lateral atomic resolution (0.2 nm) and vertical resolution (<0.01 nm)
  • Limited to conductive and semi-conductive surfaces
  • For non-conductive surfaces atomic force microscopy (AFM) is used.

Piezoelectric effect


\begin{align*} {\bf S} &= s^E\cdot {\bf T} + d\cdot{\bf E} \\ {\bf D} &= d\cdot{\bf T} + \varepsilon\cdot{\bf E} \end{align*}

  • strain: \({\bf S}\)
  • stress: \({\bf T}\)
  • electric field: \({\bf E}\)
  • displacement field: \({\bf D}\)

The strain and stress are rank 2 tensors; however, they are both symmetric. Consequently, each only has six independent components.

  • 22: 2
  • 33: 3
  • 23: 4

Force measurement

The tip oscillates above the surface of the sample. The interaction of the tip and the sample surface will cause the magnitude of the oscillation to change. The height of the cantilever is then adjusted in order to preserve the amplitude of the oscillation.
